# What are SQFT Tokens?

The Square Foot Token (SQFT) is a synthetic asset that tracks the **price per square foot** of residential real estate in a specific geography - whether that's a neighborhood, a city, or a custom region.

Each SQFT token represents one square foot of real estate in a given neighborhood. The price of each token tracks the average $/sqft of residential real estate for that neighborhood.

## How SQFT is priced

SQFT tokens are priced using a real-time oracle that aggregates:
- Recent home sale data
- Active listing prices
- Historical price trends
- Neighborhood-level square footage data

The oracle updates continuously, giving you live exposure to real estate markets without owning physical property.

## Example

<Note>
  New York currently trades at ~$761/sqft. If you trade $500 in NY SQFT tokens and prices rise 10%, your position is worth ~$550 - without ever owning property.
</Note>

## SQFT is a derivative - not ownership

SQFT tokens give you **price exposure** to real estate, not fractional ownership of a property. This is similar to how commodity futures let you gain exposure to oil prices without owning barrels of oil.

This means:
- **No property management** - you're not a landlord
- **Instant liquidity** - buy and sell anytime
- **Lower fees** - 1.5% vs 6% in traditional real estate
- **No minimum** - trade any amount ($0.10 minimum per trade)
- **Any geography** - from a specific neighborhood to an entire city

## The peg mechanism

Shareland maintains a soft peg between the oracle price (real-world $/sqft) and the market price of SQFT tokens. Market forces can push the token price slightly above or below the oracle price, but several mechanisms keep the difference small and consistent.

**If the token price rises above oracle price:** anyone can mint new tokens by locking collateral (at minimum 110% of the oracle price) and sell them at the market premium - a risk-free profit that naturally brings the price back down.

**If the token price falls below oracle price:** existing token issuers are incentivized to buy tokens at the lower market price and burn them to retrieve their locked collateral - naturally reducing supply and pushing prices back up.

This keeps SQFT token performance tightly correlated to actual real estate price movements - which is the whole point.

## Mint and Burn

Advanced users can interact directly with the Shareland protocol:

- **Mint (Borrow):** Lock up collateral (typically USDC at 110%+ of oracle price) to create new SQFT tokens. This is like borrowing tokens from the protocol.
- **Burn (Redeem):** Return previously minted tokens to retrieve your locked collateral.

Most users only need to **Trade** - buy and sell tokens on the exchange without interacting with the mint/burn mechanism.
